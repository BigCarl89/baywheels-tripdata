What is the structure of your dataset?¶

There are 295854 rentals from 01/01/2020 to 31/01/2020. Not every feature have complete data. Complete feature are: rental duration, beginning and end of rental, start station and end station coordinates (latitude and longitude), id of rented bike and user type (subscriber or customer).

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I want to figure the main use of bike rental by people looking at day and hour of usage and possibly other characteristics.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

Having complete data of date/time will help finding the trends of rentals. Also, coordinates can give useful geographical insights.

Discuss the distribution(s) of your variable(s) of interest. Were there any unusual points? Did you need to perform any transformations?¶

The distribution of my variables of interest is pretty clear, with no trasformation needed to understand them.

Of the features you investigated, were there any unusual distributions? Did you perform any operations on the data to tidy, adjust, or change the form of the data? If so, why did you do this?¶

The rental duration distribution had some outliers and i had to limit the distribution to rentals shorter than 3600 seconds. Datas were strings and not datetime and i created columns for the day of the month, corresponding day of the week and hour of rental to do temporal analysis of the dataset.
Coordinates have some erroneous data (coordinates at (0,0), https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Null_Island) and were dropped before the geographical analysis.

Surprinsingly, in Oakland is more common that a Customer rent a BayWheel bike than a Subscriber one. With the data i have on hand it is not possible to motivate this different behaviour: maybe is just that San Francisco and San Jose users are more fidelized and oriented to a subscriber type of account (and thus a more frequent use of BayWheels bike looking at previous exploration) and Oakland users are more "new" to this service. Or maybe, users from Oakland sometimes (and not sistematically) rent bikes to go to Berkeley University and do not "suffer" of commuting because of university dormitories (note: here in Italy we have a different system for Unviersity and dormitories are rare so i'm just guessing).
